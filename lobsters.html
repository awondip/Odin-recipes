Lobsters

Description

We have iconic classics like Lobster Thermidor and Lobster Newberg, plus new twists, like sous-vide poached lobster tails and orange-scented grilled lobster.

These top-rated lobster recipes are ready to impress. We're talking elegant Valentine's Day, anniversary, or marriage proposal caliber meals here.

Ingredients

  • Live lobsters, 1 per person
  • A large pot of salted water
  • Butter
  • Knife
  • Bread for dipping into lobster-infusde butter (potional)
  • lemon wedges and parsley sprigs for garnish

Steps

  1. First place a large pot of salted water over high heat.

  2. Once the water comes to a boil, place whole, live lobsters head down in the water.

  3. Cook the lobsters according to the times listed below, which are based on the weight of the lobsters.

  4. After the lobsters have turned bright red and are cooked through, remove them from the boiling water.

  5. Let the lobsters cool slightly, then serve as-is or with a side of melted butter.

  6. Place your live lobsters in the freezer for about 20 minutes before you plan to cook them. This puts the lobsters to sleep, which makes them a lot easier to handle.

  7. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add 1 tablespoon of salt per gallon of water.

  8. Remove the lobsters from the boiling water. Let them cool for 5 minutes, then serve with melted butter on the side. Garnish with lemon wedges and parsley sprigs as desired.
